CausalCrash

A Hierarchical Benchmark for Evaluating Counterfactual Reasoning in Road Safety Scenarios

NeurIPS 2026 Evaluations & Datasets Track Submission #3454

Code 🤗 Dataset 📄 Paper (Coming Soon)

Abstract

Evaluating the causal and counterfactual reasoning capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) in complex, dynamic scenarios is a critical step toward reliable autonomous driving. We introduce CausalCrash, a hierarchical benchmark dataset consisting of high-severity real-world driving videos. Unlike existing datasets that focus on simple perception or what-happened QA, CausalCrash evaluates models across multiple cognitive layers: fine-grained perception and role assignment (Level 1), temporal hazard phase localization (Level 2), and deep causal/counterfactual reasoning (Level 3).

Conclusions & Insights

The dual-judge evaluation exposes crucial blind spots in current state-of-the-art vision models and emphasizes the necessity of using multiple independent judges:

  • Temporal Failure: InternVL completely missed the critical timing sequence (predicting 7.5s instead of 3.0s) and falsely categorized the collision as a generic crash rather than a rollover.
  • Inter-Judge Disagreement: While GPT-4.1-mini gave a somewhat lenient Level 1 score (7) and marked physical_logic_valid: true, Gemini 2.5 Flash was much stricter, assigning a Level 1 score of 5 and flagging the physical logic as false due to the fundamental misunderstanding of the rollover dynamics.
  • The Value of CausalCrash: This discrepancy highlights why dual-judge protocols are essential to prevent automated leniency bias when evaluating complex counterfactual reasoning.

Evaluation Pipeline

  1. 1

    Model Inference

    Vision-Language Models (e.g., GPT-4o, Gemini 2.5, InternVL) process the crash videos and output structured JSON annotations mimicking the three-tier schema.

  2. 2

    LLM-as-a-Judge Evaluation

    Advanced LLMs (GPT-4.1-mini and Gemini 2.5 Flash) compare the predicted JSONs against human ground-truth annotations across strict, lenient, and structural reasoning metrics.

  3. 3

    CausalCrash Score (CCS)

    Generation of an aggregated score that penalizes surface-level pattern matching and rewards deep temporal and counterfactual reasoning.

Key Conclusions

  • Significant Performance Degradation: We observed a consistent drop in scores from perception (L1) down to temporal reasoning (L2) across all models. Current VLMs struggle heavily with precise spatial-temporal dynamics.
  • The CausalCrash Challenge: Even the top-scoring frontier model (Qwen3.5-plus) achieves only a 5.28 / 10 on the composite CausalCrash Score. This proves that deep counterfactual reasoning in crash scenarios remains largely unsolved.
  • Judge Leniency Bias: Throughout the evaluation, GPT-4.1-mini exhibited a persistent leniency bias compared to Gemini 2.5 Flash, which underscores the absolute necessity of our Dual-Judge Evaluation Framework.
